GULAB JAMUN

Ingredients

    For the Jamun Dough (Milk Powder Version):
    1 cup milk powder
    2 tbsp all-purpose flour (maida)
    1/2 tsp baking powder
    2 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
    2 tbsp milk (add as required)
    For the Sugar Syrup:

    2 cups sugar
    2 cups water
    4 green cardamom pods (crushed)
    1 tsp rose water or rose essence
    1 tsp lemon juice

Recipe

    1. Prepare the Sugar Syrup:

    Mix sugar, water, and crushed cardamom in a pan.
    Boil and simmer until the syrup is slightly sticky but not reaching one-string consistency.
    Remove from heat, add rose water and lemon juice, and keep warm.

    2. Make the Dough:


    In a bowl, mix milk powder, flour, and baking powder well.
    Add ghee and mix until crumbly.
    Gradually add milk and gently combine to make a soft, smooth dough.
    Avoid kneading too much, as it can make jamuns hard.
    Dough should be slightly sticky but hold shape.
    For khoya dough, mash khoya and paneer well, add semolina, flour, baking powder, and cardamom, then gently mix with milk to form dough.

    3. Shape the Jamuns:


    Divide dough into small equal portions (about 20g each).
    Grease your palms lightly with ghee or oil and roll the dough pieces into smooth, crack-free balls.
    Avoid cracks to prevent breaking while frying.

    4.Frying:


    Heat ghee or oil on medium-low flame; it should be hot but not smoking.
    Test the oil by dropping a small dough piece â€“ it should slowly rise to the surface.
    Fry a few balls gently, turning slowly for even golden brown color, about 4-6 minutes.
    Avoid overcrowding.
    Remove and drain on paper towels.

    5.Soak Jamuns:


    Immediately place fried jamuns into the warm sugar syrup.
    Let them soak for at least 2-3 hours (or overnight for best softness) to absorb syrup and become juicy and tender.

Ras Malai

Ingredients


    Full-fat milk (for making fresh chenna/paneer)
    Acidic agent (lemon juice, vinegar, or curd) to curdle milk
    Sugar (for syrup and sweetened milk)
    Cardamom powder and saffron strands (for flavoring)
    Nuts (almonds, pistachios, cashews) for garnish
    Optional: rose water or kewra essence for aromatic flavor.

Recipe

    Step 1: Preparing Chenna (Paneer)


    Boil full-fat milk and curdle it by adding lemon juice or vinegar gradually while stirring.
    Once curdled, drain using a muslin cloth,
    rinse with cold water to remove sourness and stop cooking.
    Squeeze out excess water but keep the chenna slightly moist and soft.
    Hang the cloth for 30 to 60 minutes to drain further whey.
    Knead the chenna gently until smooth and non-sticky;
    avoid over-kneading to prevent hardness.

    Step 2: Shaping and Cooking the Chenna Discs.


    Divide the kneaded chenna into equal parts,
    roll into balls,
    and gently flatten into discs.
    Prepare a boiling sugar syrup (water and sugar,
    flavored with cardamom).
    Add the chenna discs carefully into the boiling syrup,
    cover and cook on medium flame for about 7 to 10 minutes until they puff up and double in size.
    Remove and let them rest in the syrup for 20-30 minutes,
    then gently squeeze out excess syrup.

    Step 3: Preparing the Rabri (Flavored Thickened Milk)


    Boil full-fat milk in a heavy pan, stirring continuously and scraping malai (cream) from the sides.
    Cook until the milk reduces to approximately half its original volume, forming rabri.
    Add sugar, saffron, cardamom powder, and soaked saffron milk.
    Stir in chopped nuts and optionally rose water or kewra essence for aroma.

    Step 4: Assembling Rasmalai


    Soak the cooked and syrup-squeezed chenna discs into the warm rabri.
    Let them absorb the flavored milk for at least 4 hours in the refrigerator before serving.
    Garnish with slivered pistachios and almonds, and serve chilled.

    Tips for Perfect Rasmalai :


    Use full-fat or whole milk for creamy texture and richness.
    Adding ice-cold water immediately after curdling stops further cooking, keeping chenna soft.
    Do not over-knead chenna; just smooth it enough to remove graininess.
    The sugar syrup should be boiling when you add chenna discs for even puffing.
    Cook chenna discs covered to trap steam and keep them soft.
    Simmer rasgulla discs in rabri only for a couple of minutes; overcooking makes them hard or break.
    Soaking in rabri for several hours enhances flavor and juiciness.
    You can use store-bought fresh rasgulla as a shortcut by squeezing out syrup and soaking them in flavored milk.
